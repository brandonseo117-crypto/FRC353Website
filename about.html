About our team

A bit about us

Turning interest into action

Here at POBots, we’re committed to investing our expertise and resources into our community. Since 1999, we’ve supported local schools, businesses, and families through outreach and hands-on learning. Each year, we build a robot, compete in the FIRST Robotics Competition, and mentor younger teams while sharing STEM with the community.

Why we do it

Why the POBots matter

This club is more than just building a robot. It is about learning new skills, working with others, and having fun while doing it. What makes the POBots special is that the club is run by students, and there is something here for everyone.

Mechanical

The core of our robot, handling the machinery and moving parts.

Programming

The programmers manage the robot’s control systems and autonomous movement.

Electrical

Our electrical team connects the robot to the computer interface and keeps it functional.

Community Service & Awards

This group handles outreach, service projects, and award applications.

PR & Marketing

This team promotes the club through banners, flyers, social media, and website content.

Analytics & Strategy

This group studies data and team performance to improve decisions and competition strategy.

Our history

Built on teamwork since 1999

The POBots was first established in 1999 under the leadership of Mr. Frank Saladino at Plainview-Old Bethpage John F. Kennedy High School in Plainview, New York. Ever since then, our club has grown to become a staple not only in the school district, but throughout the entire community.
